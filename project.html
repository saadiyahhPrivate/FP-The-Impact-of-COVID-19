The Impact of COVID-19

We are currently in the thick of a pandemic that has drastically altered various aspects of life as we know it.

There have been extensive visualizations in the media on the spread of the disease, mortality rates, and various other cold metrics.

However, relatively less attention has been paid to the effects of the outbreak that are felt on a more personal level.

The project aims to address this by providing a number of visualizations that examine a selection of these repercussions.



Scroll down to start exploring!

Mobility

The explosive global spread of COVID-19 around mid-March has left almost every country scrambling to stop the virus in its tracks. As a result of the "social distancing" guidelines, foot traffic for public venues and events has effectively ceased.

Mobility trends for the five countries with the highest number of cumulative cases (as of 05/13/2020) are shown below. Residential areas have seen a general increase in traffic, while activity for other categories that are more public in nature has dropped dramatically.

World School Closures

In an attempt to reduce the rate of spread of COVID-19 and "flatten the curve", countries across the world have gradually mandated school closures.

Although some countries recommended partial school closures in affected areas (light blue), in many cases these recommendations soon changed into generalized nationwide school closures (dark blue). Other countries immediately closed schools nationwide.

Note: Countries that do not have a tooltip attached are not represented in the dataset.

State School Closures

As it became clear that packed classrooms were an unsafe environment for students, US states began shutting down their schools, many of them ahead of any official Stay-At-Home Order.

Restaurant traffic

A state of national emergency was declared in the United States on March 13th. Subsequently, countless restaurants have suffered losses in sales and have had to temporarily suspend their business or find alternate measures to continue serving.

Hover over each state to see the state-level graph.

Flight Volume data

As more countries started to restrict non-essential travel or even ban international flights altogether, the aviation industry has seen a severe decline in traffic.

The flight volume graph below shows the number of daily commercial flights (Commercial passenger flights, cargo flights, charter flights, some business jet flights) and all flights (commercial flights + more business jet flights, private flights, gliders, most helicopter flights, most ambulance flights, government flights, some military flights, drones).

As of mid-March, we see a sharp decline in the number of daily flights across the world.

Traffic Collisions in LA County

One upside of the decrease in mobility for non-essential activities is the decrease in traffic collisions across the nation.

For instance, the daily number of reported traffic collisions in Los Angeles County is typically around 140 collisions. After the Stay-At-Home Order that took into effect on March 19, 2020, the daily number of reported traffic collisions decreased drastically to around 60 collisions.

With less accidents happening on the road, more resources can be allocated towards fighting COVID-19!
